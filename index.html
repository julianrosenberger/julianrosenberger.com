Julian Rosenberger

PhD Researcher in Interpretable Machine Learning & HCI

I am a PhD researcher at the University of Regensburg and TU Dresden, bridging the gap between technical AI capabilities and human understanding.

My work focuses on Interpretable Machine Learning (IML) and Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). I combine computational benchmarking with cognitive user studies to build transparent, trustworthy systems for high-stakes domains like healthcare and HR.

Focus Areas

Interpretable ML Explainable AI (XAI) Human-AI Interaction Generalized Additive Models (GAMs) NLP User Experiments

Selected Publications

Navigating the Rashomon Effect ECIS 2025
Designed adaptive interfaces that tailor interpretable model explanations to individual user cognitive styles to resolve model multiplicity.
Quantifying Visual Properties of GAM Shape Plots HICSS 2025
Developed objective metrics to predict the cognitive load of visual AI explanations, establishing a framework for measuring interpretability.
Understanding Data-Sharing with AI Systems PAJAIS (2026, Conditionally Accepted)
Investigated dual-process dynamics in AI interactions; found that transparency's effectiveness in fostering data-sharing is contingent on pre-existing trust.
CareerBERT: Matching Resumes to Jobs Expert Systems with Applications (2025)
Architected a BERT-based recommender system for high-stakes HR domains; addressed fairness and transparency challenges in deploying LLMs.
