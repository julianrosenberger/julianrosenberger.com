Julian Rosenberger

PhD Researcher in Human-AI Decision-Making

Investigating why explainable AI often fails to improve human decisions, and how to fix it.

Despite advances in XAI, challenges like automation bias, over-reliance, and mistrust persist. I combine large-scale behavioral experiments (N=200+) with technical ML implementation to understand these failures and design AI systems that genuinely align with human cognition and foster appropriate reliance.

Selected Papers

Navigating the Rashomon Effect ECIS 2025
Designed adaptive interfaces that tailor interpretable model explanations to individual user cognitive styles, resolving model multiplicity challenges in human-AI interaction.
Quantifying Visual Properties of GAM Shape Plots HICSS 2025
Developed objective metrics to predict the cognitive load of visual AI explanations, establishing a quantitative framework for measuring interpretability.
Understanding Data-Sharing with AI Systems PAJAIS (In Press)
Investigated dual-process dynamics in AI interactions; found that transparency's effectiveness in fostering data-sharing is contingent on pre-existing trust levels.
Leveraging Interpretable ML in Intensive Care Ann. Oper. Res. (2024)
Demonstrated that GAMs match black-box performance with <15 features in ICU prediction; validated approach with medical experts using MIMIC-III data.
CareerBERT: Matching Resumes to Jobs Expert Syst. Appl. (2025)
Co-architected a BERT-based recommender system for high-stakes HR decisions; addressed fairness and transparency challenges in deploying LLMs for recruitment.
